Privacy Policy

Effective: May 9, 2026   ·   Last updated: May 9, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what data the LockOut iOS app ("LockOut", "we", "us") collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. LockOut is published by Brian Coleman as an independent developer.

Questions? Email brianpcoleman@gmail.com.

TL;DR

  • We collect only what's needed to run the app: your email, name (or display name), a Firebase user ID, and gameplay data (streaks, exercises completed, achievements).
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites. There are no advertising SDKs and no analytics that profile you.
  • Camera and motion data used for exercise detection never leaves your device.
  • You can delete your account and all associated data from inside the app at any time (Settings → Delete Account).

1. What we collect

1.1 Account information

When you create an account using Sign in with Apple, Google Sign-In, or email + password, we collect:

  • Email address (from your sign-in provider or what you type)
  • Name or display name (from your sign-in provider, or one you choose in the app)
  • Firebase user ID — an opaque identifier we use to associate your gameplay data with your account

1.2 Gameplay and product-interaction data

To provide app functionality (streaks, achievements, leaderboards, friends), we store:

  • Total elixir, weekly elixir, current streak, longest streak
  • Exercise records (which exercises you completed, total reps by exercise type, personal bests)
  • Schedules you've configured (which apps you've chosen to block and when)
  • Unlocked achievements
  • Streak freezes available
  • The display name you've chosen
  • Last update timestamps

1.3 Friends and leaderboards

If you use the friends feature, we store:

  • A 6-character friend code you can share with others
  • Your friends list (the user IDs and display names of people you've added)
  • Friend requests sent to or received from you

Your display name, current streak, and total elixir are visible to your friends on their leaderboard. Nothing else from your profile is shared.

1.4 Crash and diagnostic data

The app uses Firebase, which may collect basic crash and diagnostic information automatically. This data is not linked to your identity for our use, and we use it only to keep the app stable.

1.5 Camera and motion data (NOT collected)

LockOut uses your device's camera and motion sensors to detect exercises (push-ups, squats, jumping jacks). This processing happens entirely on your device. No camera images, video, or motion readings are uploaded to our servers, stored remotely, or shared with anyone.

1.6 Screen Time / Family Controls data

LockOut uses Apple's Family Controls and DeviceActivity frameworks to block apps you choose. Apple gives the app opaque tokens representing your selections — we never see the actual app names, your usage history, or which apps are installed on your device. Apple manages this data; it is not transmitted to us.

2. How we use the data

We use the data above only to:

  • Authenticate you (sign you in to your account)
  • Save your gameplay state across devices and re-installs
  • Show you on leaderboards alongside friends you've added
  • Process friend requests and let friends find you by friend code
  • Improve app stability (via crash reports)

We do not use any data for advertising, profiling, or to build a profile of you across other apps or websites.

4. Tracking

LockOut does not track you across other apps and websites, and our app's privacy manifest declares this. We do not use the App Tracking Transparency framework because we have nothing to track.

There are no advertising SDKs, no analytics SDKs that profile users, and no third-party tracking domains. Our PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy manifest in the app bundle is the source of truth for this declaration.

5. Data retention

We retain your account and gameplay data for as long as your account exists. When you delete your account (see section 7), we delete:

  • Your record in our lockout_users collection (and all subcollections: exercises, schedules)
  • Your record in our lockout_leaderboard collection
  • Your Firebase Authentication account
  • Any Google Sign-In session on your device

This deletion is permanent and immediate.

Crash logs and diagnostic data may persist for up to 90 days in Firebase's logs before automatic deletion.

6. Children

LockOut is rated 4+ on the App Store but is intended for adults and parents who want to manage their own or their family's screen time. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 in jurisdictions that require it).

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has created an account without your consent, email brianpcoleman@gmail.com and we will delete the account and associated data.

7. Your rights and choices

7.1 Delete your account and data

Open LockOut → Settings → Delete Account. This permanently deletes everything described in section 5.

7.2 Access, correct, or export

To request a copy of your data, ask us to correct it, or request deletion outside of the in-app flow, email brianpcoleman@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

7.3 Permissions

You can revoke camera, motion, notification, and Screen Time / Family Controls permissions at any time in iOS Settings → LockOut.

7.4 GDPR (EU/EEA/UK users)

If you are in the EU, EEA, UK, or Switzerland, the legal basis for processing your data is:

  • Performance of a contract — for account creation, gameplay sync, and friends/leaderboards (we cannot provide these features without the data).
  • Legitimate interests — for crash diagnostics (keeping the app stable).

You have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, port, or object to processing of your personal data. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. Contact brianpcoleman@gmail.com to exercise any of these rights.

7.5 California (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete it, the right to correct it, and the right to opt out of any sale or sharing of personal information. We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined under the CPRA. To exercise any right, email brianpcoleman@gmail.com.

8. Security

We rely on Google Firebase's industry-standard security (encryption in transit and at rest, authenticated database rules) for cloud-stored data. No system is perfectly secure; if we ever become aware of a breach affecting your data, we will notify you in compliance with applicable law.

9. International transfers

Your data is stored on Google Firebase servers, primarily in the United States. By using the app from outside the United States, you consent to your data being transferred to and processed in the United States.

For EU/EEA/UK users, transfers rely on Google's Standard Contractual Clauses where applicable.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated in-app or via the email associated with your account. The "Last updated" date at the top of this policy reflects the current version.
